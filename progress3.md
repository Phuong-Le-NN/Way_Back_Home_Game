2. What feedback did you receive on your last progress report? How did you incorporate that feedback?

        One of the main pieces of feedback that we recieved from the second progress report is that we need to update our asset.md file. In order to not be completely scrambling at the very end, we need to add all of the assets that we used to the md file. 
        
        There were a couple pieces of important feedback from the in class game demos, that we thougth was important to take note of here:
        
        The first was incorporating the story more so into the game and maybe adding instruction pages between each of the levels in order to give the player more context. In order to address this, Annie added instruction panels before each level that explained a bit of the story and how to make it through the level. We thought that these acted as good check points in order to stay engaged with the player and keep them on track.
        
        We also got questions about the underworld level, and how it was going to contribute to the story and how it might change the ending. We weren't originally going to keep the level, but when we were working with our composer, he had mentioned that he really enjoyed the level and thought it was an interesting part of the game. Therefore, we deciced to put the underground level between the locked door level and the enemy level. We incorporated this into the story by telling the player that the dog had fallen into the hole. 
        
        There were also questions surrounding the background and platforms - was the dog climbing a mountain, etc? Thinking about the platform assets in the game and how they contribute to the story was an important piece of feedback that we recieved. We wanted to keep the platforms the same on each of the different levels in order to have uniformity throughout the game. What we did add however was a level of randomness to the layout of the platforms on each of the levels. This helps each of the jump maps not be the same each time the player plays the game as well. We wanted the game to be sort of a quest through the woods vibe and we thought that the platform we chose went along with the theme. 
        
        Regarding having the different character options and their different abiltiies, we ultimately decided to keep them somewhat balanced because the two players will have different advantages, such as one of them will be fast but not able to jump as high, and the other one will have the opposite. 
        
        Additionally, we got a reccomendation that we should label the health bar in order to make it more noticeable for the player on the screen. To address this, we decided to add a label to the healthbar in each level.
        
        Finally, we got the question of if there will be squireels in the underworld, and to answer this - no there will not be. We wanted to keep this mechanic of the game uniquely to that level.
